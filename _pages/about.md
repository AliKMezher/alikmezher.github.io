Welcome! I am Ali Mezher, a mathematician and researcher affiliated with **The University of Texas at Austin**. 

My research is primarily focused on **mathematical physics**, with a specific emphasis on **quantum electrodynamics (QED)** and the rigorous analysis of **partial differential equations (PDEs)**. 

### Research Interests
I am deeply interested in the mathematical foundations of physical systems. My recent and ongoing work involves:
* **Bose-Einstein Condensation:** Investigating the dynamical formation, energy decay, and macroscopic ground state occupation of BECs.
* **Quantum Electrodynamics:** Analyzing resonant decay and the rigorous application of unitary dilation operators on Fock spaces.
* **Rigorous PDE Analysis:** Exploring the behavior of wave functions, including non-conserved $L^2$ norms in specific time-dependent systems.

### Recent News
* **[April 2026]** Currently finalizing a manuscript on the dynamical formation of Bose-Einstein Condensation for submission.
* **[Spring 2026]** Involved in course preparation and grading for Linear Algebra at UT Austin.

### Beyond Mathematics
I am an advocate for efficient academic workflows. I specialize in advanced LaTeX typesetting (frequently utilizing Overleaf and VSCode with BibTeX) and have a strong interest in open-source development, Linux environments, and optimizing technical setups for mathematical research.
